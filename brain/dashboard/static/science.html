The reward signal is computed in engine._compute_health_reward() from kernel performance metrics and consciousness analytics. It is a composite of 7 terms:

$$R = R_{\text{latency}} + R_{\text{backlog}} + R_{\text{overrun}} + R_{\text{slow}} + R_{\text{reasoning}} + R_{\text{confidence}} - R_{\text{overconf}}$$

Training Loop — Advantage-Weighted Imitation Learning

The policy NN is trained via advantage-weighted imitation learning, not standard policy gradient. Key innovation: when the NN's own past proposal coincided with positive advantage, it trains toward its own action rather than the kernel's, breaking the "perfect copy → ties forever" equilibrium.

$$\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{B}\sum_{i=1}^{B} w_i \cdot \| \hat{\mathbf{a}}_i - \mathbf{a}^*_i \|^2$$

Where the target action $\mathbf{a}^*_i$ and weight $w_i$ are:

$$\mathbf{a}^*_i = \begin{cases} \mathbf{a}^{\text{nn}}_i & \text{if } A_i > \tau_{\text{adv}} \text{ and nn\_action exists} \\ \mathbf{a}^{\text{kernel}}_i & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$
$$A_i = r_i - \bar{r}_{\text{EMA}}, \quad \hat{A}_i = A_i / \max(\sigma_r, 0.01)$$
$$w_i = \text{clamp}(1 + \hat{A}_i, \; 0.1, \; 3.0)$$

Shadow A/B Evaluation

The NN runs in shadow mode alongside the kernel. It proposes actions without applying them, then scores retrospectively when the conversation outcome arrives. The scoring is outcome-based with noop detection and diversity bonuses.

▶ Retrospective Scoring Algorithm policy/evaluator.py

For each pending shadow proposal (max age 120s):

  1. Compute noop detection: if $\max(|\Delta_{tw}|) < 2.5\sigma$ AND $L_2(\Delta_{tw}) < 5\sigma$ AND budget diff ≤ 3 AND mode/response unchanged → noop
  2. For noops: $R_{\text{nn}} = R_{\text{actual}} + \text{NOOP\_PENALTY}$, kernel gets $R_{\text{actual}}$
  3. For non-noops, compute deviation magnitude: $d = \sqrt{\sum \delta_i^2}$ and scaled bonus $\beta = 0.08 \cdot \min(d / 0.15, 1)$
  4. Reward improved ($\Delta r > 0.02$): NN gets $+\beta$
  5. Reward degraded ($\Delta r < -0.02$): kernel gets $+0.5\beta$
  6. Stable + healthy ($r > 0.7$): NN gets $+0.5\beta$ (diversity bonus)
  7. Stable + struggling ($r > 0.1$): NN gets $+0.3\beta$ (reduced diversity)

Classification into win/loss/tie:

$$\text{margin} = R_{\text{nn}} - R_{\text{kernel}}$$
$$\text{class} = \begin{cases} \text{nn\_win} & \text{if } \text{margin} > 0.03 \\ \text{kernel\_win} & \text{if } \text{margin} < -0.03 \\ \text{tie} & \text{if } |\text{margin}| < 0.01 \text{ OR } |\text{margin}| \leq 0.03 \end{cases}$$

Fidelity-weighted loss: Each teacher signal carries a fidelity score $f \in [0, 1]$. Signals below 0.3 are quarantined. The loss is weighted by fidelity:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{distill}} = f \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{base}}(\hat{y}, y_{\text{teacher}})$$

Approximator features from any audio_features* source (including audio_features_enriched, a 32-dim mixed-scale vector combining 7 spectral features with 25-dim ECAPA-TDNN speaker embeddings) are z-score normalized before training: $x' = (x - \mu) / \max(\sigma, \epsilon)$. Without normalization, large-scale spectral features would dominate gradients and suppress learning on the ECAPA range.

Accuracy gating: Post-train accuracy below TIER1_MIN_ACCURACY (5%) increments a failure counter. After 3 consecutive sub-floor results, the specialist is disabled for the session. Cold-start overfit guard: DISTILLATION_REGRESSION_MIN_SAMPLES = 50 — the regression check is skipped when the specialist has fewer than 50 paired training samples. This prevents initial-build memorization (100% accuracy on 15 samples) from creating an unreachable regression baseline that triggers perpetual rollbacks.

Dependency-retrain ordering: Some specialists declare depends_on another specialist (e.g. emotion_depth depends on speaker_repr because its audio_features_enriched input contains ECAPA embeddings). To avoid training on stale encoder output, a dependent is skipped if its dependency retrained within DISTILLATION_DEP_RETRAIN_WINDOW_S = 60s. Critical scheduling detail: the check uses a snapshot of _tier1_last_retrain_time taken once at the start of _run_distillation_cycle, not the live dict. Without the snapshot, a dependency retraining earlier in the same cycle would stamp its timestamp and cause its dependents to self-block inside the same tick, producing the "healthy inputs, zero epochs" pathology observed on emotion_depth pre-fix. Cross-cycle protection (dependency retrained in a previous cycle within the window) still holds via the same snapshot read.

Brier Score & Expected Calibration Error

Standard statistical measures of confidence calibration quality, computed in epistemic/calibration/confidence_calibrator.py.

$$\text{Brier} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}(p_i - o_i)^2$$

Where $p_i$ is the predicted confidence and $o_i \in \{0, 1\}$ is the observed outcome. Lower is better; 0 = perfect calibration.

$$\text{ECE} = \sum_{b=1}^{B} \frac{|B_b|}{N} \cdot |\bar{p}_b - \bar{o}_b|$$

Where $B = 5$ bins, $\bar{p}_b$ is mean predicted confidence in bin $b$, and $\bar{o}_b$ is observed accuracy in bin $b$. Bins are $[0, 0.2), [0.2, 0.4), \ldots, [0.8, 1.0]$.

Overconfidence detection: If $\bar{p}_b > \bar{o}_b$ across multiple bins → system is overconfident. This feeds back as a negative term in the reward function ($-0.15 \cdot \min(\text{oc}, 0.25)$) and triggers belief confidence adjustments (max ±0.05 per adjustment, bounded deque of 200).

Biometric EMA Smoothing

Both speaker and face recognition use Exponential Moving Average smoothing to reduce single-frame volatility in similarity scores.

$$s_t^{\text{smooth}} = \alpha \cdot s_t^{\text{raw}} + (1 - \alpha) \cdot s_{t-1}^{\text{smooth}}$$

L3 Escalation Governance — Evidence Class Separation (Phase 6.5)

Phase 6.5 formalises the control-theoretic distinction between ever-proven capability and present-state readiness. The system maintains five explicit evidence fields instead of a single confidence scalar, so the rules that gate L3 activation can be stated as predicate logic over disjoint inputs rather than thresholds over a blended truth flag.

Five-Field State Model

Let $\mathcal{E}$ denote the set of live policy evaluations and $\mathcal{A}$ denote the attestation ledger (hash-verified, operator-seeded). Define:

$$ \begin{aligned} c_{\text{ok}} &= \text{check\_promotion\_eligibility}(\mathcal{E}_t) \in \{0,1\} \\ p_{\text{ok}} &= \mathbb{1}\!\left[\exists\, r \in \mathcal{A} :\; r.\text{capability}=\text{autonomy.l3} \;\land\; r.\text{artifact\_status} \in \{\text{hash\_verified},\text{missing}\}\right] \\ r_{\text{ok}} &= c_{\text{ok}} \lor p_{\text{ok}} \\ a_{\text{ok}} &= c_{\text{ok}} \land \mathbb{1}[\text{level}_t \geq 3] \end{aligned} $$

and $\text{approval\_required} \equiv \text{true}$ as a policy constant. The cache builder computes $c_{\text{ok}}$ from $\mathcal{E}_t$ at snapshot time; no code path derives $c_{\text{ok}}$ from any persisted file. This is the no-backfill invariant and it is the single rule that makes the distinction between "ever" and "now" structurally honest.

Intent

Holographic Reduced Representations (HRR) — a Vector-Symbolic Architecture (VSA) — allow JARVIS to represent a relation like $\texttt{subject} \otimes \texttt{relation} \otimes \texttt{object}$ inside a single fixed-width vector, then superpose many such relations into one compact "mental scene" vector. Core Operations (Circular-Convolution HRR)

Let $\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y} \in \mathbb{R}^d$ be unit-norm symbol vectors, $\mathcal{F}$ the FFT, and $\overline{\cdot}$ the complex conjugate.

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{bind:} \quad \mathbf{x} \circledast \mathbf{y} &= \mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\bigl(\mathcal{F}(\mathbf{x}) \odot \mathcal{F}(\mathbf{y})\bigr) \\ \text{unbind (conjugate):} \quad \mathbf{b} \oslash \mathbf{k} &= \mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\bigl(\mathcal{F}(\mathbf{b}) \odot \overline{\mathcal{F}(\mathbf{k})}\bigr) \\ \text{superpose:} \quad \text{sup}(\mathbf{v}_{1..n}) &= \text{proj}\!\left(\textstyle\sum_{i=1}^n \mathbf{v}_i\right) \\ \text{similarity:} \quad s(\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}) &= \cos(\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}) \end{aligned} $$

Unbind uses conjugate correlation, not spectral division $\mathcal{F}(\mathbf{b}) / \mathcal{F}(\mathbf{k})$. Division is unstable when the key spectrum has small components; conjugate correlation is the approximate inverse and is the standard HRR choice.

Role-Filler Fact Encoding

A fact is encoded as a superposition of role-filler bindings, then projected:

$$ \text{FACT} = \text{proj}\!\bigl( \mathbf{r}_\text{SUBJ} \circledast \mathbf{s} \;+\; \mathbf{r}_\text{REL} \circledast \mathbf{r} \;+\; \mathbf{r}_\text{OBJ} \circledast \mathbf{o} \;+\; \mathbf{r}_\text{CTX} \circledast \mathbf{c} \;+\; \mathbf{r}_\text{TIME} \circledast \mathbf{t} \bigr) $$

Each symbol vector is deterministic in $(\text{namespace}, \text{name}, \text{seed}, d)$: the same symbol produces an identical vector across restarts without any on-disk symbol store. Different symbols are near-orthogonal by construction (random projection in high $d$).

$$ \text{cleanup}(\hat{\mathbf{s}}; \mathcal{D}) = \operatorname*{argmax}_{\mathbf{d} \in \mathcal{D}} \cos(\hat{\mathbf{s}}, \mathbf{d}) \quad \text{if}\; \max_\mathbf{d} \cos \geq \theta $$
